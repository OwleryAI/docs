Keeping tracking up to date helps your shippers stay informed and reduces check-call volume. Here's how to update your loads as they move.

<Note>
  All times are shown in the facility's local time zone. Saved times can be edited but not removed, and actual dates cannot be set in the future.
</Note>

## Enter stop times

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the load">
    In the left sidebar, click **Shipments (Loads)** and select a load to open the drawer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the Tracking tab">
    Click the **Tracking** tab. You'll see each stop listed with two fields under **Actual**:

    - **Actual Arrived** — when the driver arrived at the facility.
    - **Actual Departed** — when the driver left after loading or unloading.

    {/*  📸 SCREENSHOT: The Tracking tab inside the load drawer, showing at least two stops (pickup and delivery) with the Actual Arrived and Actual Departed fields visible. Ideally show one stop already filled in and one empty.  */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your times">
    Enter the actual arrival and departure times for each stop, then save.

    - Saving times on the **pickup** stop updates the shipment status to **In Progress**.
    - Saving times on the **final delivery** stop marks the shipment as **Delivered**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Log in-transit updates

Between stops, you can use the dropdown on the Tracking tab to log additional updates:

- **Location Update** — share the driver's current position.
- **Delay** — flag that the shipment is running behind.
- **Exception** — report an issue (breakdown, weather, detention, etc.).

These updates are optional, but they go a long way toward keeping your shipper relationships strong.
